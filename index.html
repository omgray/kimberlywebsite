Meet The Therapist:

About Me:

Kimberly McCray, a Licensed Mental Health Counselor based in South Florida, brings over 7 years of expertise. Holding a Bachelors degree in Sociology from the University of South Florida and a Masters degree in Counseling with a concentration in Mental Health from Nova Southeastern University, Kimberly employs a collaborative, strength-based approach. She works to empower clients, allowing them to be the authors of their own lives. Emphasizing the significance of the therapeutic relationship, Kimberly values building rapport with individuals and supporting their families.

My Passion:

As a kidney transplant recipient, Kimberly is passionately dedicated to promoting healing in those grappling with chronic illnesses. Recognizing the complexities and challenges of navigating limitations imposed by chronic illness, she strives to help individuals normalize their experiences in a way that maintains connections with others. Rejecting the idea that an individual should identify solely with their disorder, she sees it as just one facet among many other important parts of who they are.

Areas of Practice:


Chronic Illness:

Therapy for chronic illnesses can offer substantial benefits to individuals contending with prolonged diseases, chronic pain, and other enduring conditions. Chronic illnesses, characterized by their long-term nature, persist over an individual's lifetime. These conditions typically arise from intricate causes and may result in impairment, pain, or disability, influencing a person's perspective on the world and altering their thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. Living with chronic illnesses can limit engagement in school or work, impact relationships, and pose challenges to regular activities.

Life Transitions:

Life is an ongoing sequence of transitions. Navigating change can be challenging, as even positive life shifts often induce stress. An adjustment disorder emerges as a potent emotional or behavioral response to stress or trauma, resulting in short-term symptoms that may elicit more intense reactions than usual. This can manifest in easily triggered tears, feelings of depression and hopelessness, or engaging in risky, reckless, or impulsive behaviors. The specific behaviors and emotions experienced vary from person to person.

Depression:

Depressive disorder goes beyond experiencing sadness or facing a difficult phase; it represents a significant mental health condition. The encouraging news is that it is treatable. Depression ranks among the most prevalent mental disorders in the US and, for certain individuals, may lead to impairments restricting their ability to perform essential life activities. This condition introduces various emotional and physical challenges, impacting one's effectiveness in both work and home environments.
